Marking time in the Valley of the Kings

Pyramids, Tombs and Ancient Artifacts

In 2013, one of the worlds oldest sundials was discovered by an archeological team led by Professor Susanne Bickel from the University of Basel. The discovery took place in Egypt's Nile River Valley and dates back to the 13th century B.C. LIVESCIENCE. Made from a flattened piece of limestone the sundials design is a black semicircle divided into 12 sections. The object is thought to have been used to visualize the length of hours.
